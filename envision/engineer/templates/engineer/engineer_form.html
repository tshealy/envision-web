Request For Proposal

Recreational Park and Holding Facility for Reclaimed Wastewater

The local city government hired your firm to construct a new recreational park. The purpose of the park is dual purpose, to redevelop an existing underused site and hold treated wastewater. The treated wastewater does not meet standards for drinking, but it can help replenish depleting ground aquifers. You should act as the sustainability engineer: review the project's scope of work and recommend how sustainability credits from the Envision rating system could be achieved. For each credit, if you select a level of achievement above zero, please include a short explanation.

Purpose

This region of the country is arid. Local freshwater supplies are limited and only with large capital expenditure. Large quantities of freshwater can be saved by wastewater reuse and recycling, reducing environmental pollution and improving carbon footprint. Reuse can be an alternative water supply option. The purpose of this project is to construct a facility to hold reclaimed wastewater. The reclaimed water is meant to help replenish the groundwater aquifers.

Project Details

The 9-acre park will help transform an underutilized part of the city and provide a "green" space for residents. The park will also serve to hold and treat reclaimed wastewater and stormwater. This reclaimed water will provide irrigation to the facility and help replenish ground aquifers that are receding at an unprecedented rate. Below is a list of aspects the project should provide:

  • Multi-purpose fields and walking trails
  • Adjacent parking lots
  • Restroom and community facilities
  • Landscaping to treat water and provide shading
  • Reclaimed wastewater holding facility for 680,000 gallons per day
  • Preserving and restoring existing habitats and important natural resources
  • Locally sourced materials

Rendering

The project will include construction of trails, two pedestrian bridge structures over the water, interpretive signs, picnic and seating areas, an asphalt or concrete parking area, and firelane around the south building. The south building will be renovated in the future and used as a community center for the park.

Current Phase

The water will enter an on-site pre-treatment unit and a new pump station that will pump the water through two new force main pipelines along the north side of the site to the east side of the site. Water will exit the pipelines at the east side of the site through a water feature and submerged manifold headworks. Water will flow westward across the landscape zones.
