Welcome to the Envision Case Study Training Program!

The purpose of this training is to provide real-world examples of how The Envision Rating System for Sustainable Infrastructure is used to design and assess infrastructure projects. Below is a case study about Cordova, AL. To begin the training read through the documentation.

Phoenix, AZ

The city of Phoenix, AZ hired your firm to construct a new park. You should act as the sustainability engineer. Review the project's scope of work and recommend how sustainability credits from the Envision rating system could be achieved. For each credit, if you select a level of achievement pleas include a short explanation. The higher the level of achievement the longer the explanation required.

Scope of Work and Deliverables

Below is a list of all the aspects that are part of the project design also illustrated in Figure 2.

  • A 17.9-acre park with recreational amenities
  • An 8-foot wide trail
  • Fenced-in dog parks
  • A multi-purpose playing field
  • Two new parking lots
  • Picnic shelters
  • A playground with benches
  • Restroom facilities
  • Associated landscaping
  • Storm-water management
  • Preserving existing habitats and important natural resources
  • Locally sourced materials
